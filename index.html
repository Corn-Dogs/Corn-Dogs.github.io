WHO IS NICHOLAS DIXON?

Nicholas Dixon is a 6"3 tall women's right activist who loves to read feminist literature. Dixon is a rather peculiar creature, as he lives on a little hut on a rainbow. His colleagues often call him Leprochaun Man. This was before they all mysteriously dissapeared. Dixon has claimed to have not seen anything on the night of, and has denied all allegations.

Born in 1984 in Chicago, Illinouis, Dixon loves the little things in life and long walks on the beach. He especially enjoys smelling the roses on the way back to his rainbow shack. Most recently, he has gained an interest in photography. His most favourite thing to take photos of is nature. Rumour has it that he visited Whistler, BC during the summer of '24 with his family and took many photos of nature, specifically of the stars. On one late night (Approximately August 1st at 12:32 A.M.), Dixon and his brother went out to take photos of the stars, using a method that they had acquired on social media previous day. With this website, I hope to be able to enlighten you to the photography work of Nicholas Dixon, to highlight how he truly reaches for the stars.

Here are a few of his recent works

This is a photo of his cat: Cleo. An absolute fridge of a cat, Cleo weighs in at 300lbs. At the time this photo was taken, she was lounging in the sun on the dining table and living room chair.

This photo was taken on Nicholas' Auntie's (mother's sister) back porch. That night, they ate Deliveroo Domino's Pizza and watched The Hangover on a projector they had set up outside. The flimsy homeade projector screen barely held up because of the wind, but they set up books and stools to attempt keep it rooted. In the end, when the movie was over, everyone was curled up in blankets and watched the stars.

This photo was taken in Whisler with his brother at 12:32 A.M. They were jetlagged. Method: Long exposure mode at night.

This photo was taken in Whistler with his brother at 12:35 A.M. Unfortunately, the road was the only place with the gap in the trees to see the stars. Nearly 10 people almost hit them with their cars.

This last photo is my personal favourite. It shows Nicholas and his family members frolocking in the fields with dandilions. It is very bright and cheerful.
